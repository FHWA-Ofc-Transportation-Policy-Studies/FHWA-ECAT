Overview -  The Inequity Identification Tool (IIT) is an interactive mapping application to highlight highway externalities, such as noise and air pollution, and who they affect. In addition to featuring road segments with estimates of the magnitude of externality costs, the IIT includes county level equity ratios—the percent of highway externality cost borne by a demographic divided by their percent of the population within the county—for each externality type and demographic. It also includes layers such as population and demographic data at the Census block group level from the Census database, fatal crashes from the FARS database, school locations, and historic redlining maps from the ArcGIS Living Atlas. The IIT can be helpful for anyone interested in the equity of highway externality exposure. Currently, the IIT covers noise and air pollution from highways, with other modules in development. For more information on the methodology and data please see the below resources and documentation.
Disclaimer -  Due to uncertainty inherent in this type of modeling, IIT estimates should not be used in federal processes such as the National Environmental Policy Act (NEPA), conformity, State Implementation Plan (SIP), or similar State processes, and cannot be used for noise analysis as required under 23 CFR 772. This analysis only estimates PM2.5 and does not account for other emissions, relationship to standards or attainment status, or the background levels of pollution. The pollutant dispersion is modeled based on average meteorological conditions from a wide sample of locations, while specific local conditions alter the dispersion. The methodologies, data, and results presented here do not introduce any new requirements.
